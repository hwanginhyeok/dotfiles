# Data Required for Tax Filing + How to Collect It

> Used in SKILL STEP 2. Filter to only the items matching the profile and present them as a checklist.
> Core principle: **A cost with no proof = expense disallowed = higher tax.** Recovering qualified evidence is 80% of tax savings.

---

## 1. Income (Revenue) Data — "How much did you earn"

| Data | Where to collect | Notes |
|--------|--------|------|
| Electronic tax invoice sales | Hometax `My Hometax > Look up electronic tax invoices` | Business-to-business transactions |
| Electronic invoice sales (tax-exempt) | Same Hometax menu | Tax-exempt goods/services |
| Credit card sales | Hometax `Look up credit card sales data` / Credit Finance Association | POS/terminals |
| Cash receipt sales | Hometax `Look up cash receipts` | Required for mandatory-issuance business types |
| Platform settlement records | Delivery apps (Baemin, Coupang Eats), open markets (Smart Store, Coupang, 11st), PG companies | **Reported to the NTS — cannot be omitted** |
| Cash/bank transfer sales | Business account transaction history | Risk of detection if omitted |

> Hometax's **auto-fill / pre-filled service** automatically reflects much of your sales. However, platform and cash sales must be verified by you.

---

## 2. Cost (Purchase/Expense) Data — "How much did you spend" (the core of tax savings)

### The 4 types of qualified evidence (required for an expense to be recognized)
1. **Tax invoice / invoice** (business-to-business transactions)
2. **Credit card sales slip** (business card)
3. **Cash receipt** (for expense proof — issued under the business registration number)
4. **Withholding tax receipt** (labor costs)

| Cost item | Where to collect | Caution |
|-----------|--------|------|
| Purchase tax invoices | Hometax `Look up electronic tax invoices` | Core of input VAT deduction |
| Business credit card usage history | Hometax `Register/look up business credit card` | **Registering the card aggregates it automatically** |
| Cash receipts (expense proof) | Hometax `Look up cash receipts` | Not for personal income deduction — use the business one |
| Labor costs (employees) | Withholding tax filings, 4 major insurances | Payroll ledger, employment contract |
| Labor costs (freelance outsourcing) | 3.3% withholding tax receipt | Submit a payment statement |
| Rent | Lease agreement + tax invoice/financial proof | |
| Employer's share of 4 major insurances | Insurance corporation payment records | Recognized as an expense |
| Vehicle maintenance costs | Card/cash receipt, insurance/repair | Business-use ratio |
| Depreciable assets | Tax invoices for acquiring machinery, vehicles, fixtures | Depreciation over useful life |
| Interest expense | Proof of interest payments on business loans | Business-related portion only |
| Telecom/utilities/supplies | Bills/cards under the business's name | |

### Checks to prevent missed expenses
- ☐ Did you **register** your business credit card with Hometax? (If not, you must enter each item manually)
- ☐ Did you **register** your business account? (Double-entry bookkeeping filers face a penalty tax if not registered)
- ☐ Did you obtain qualified evidence for expenditures over 30,000 won? (Without qualified evidence, a 2% evidence-deficiency penalty tax applies)
- ☐ Did you file the labor cost payment statement and withholding tax? (If not, the expense is denied + penalty tax)

## 3. Additional Data for Comprehensive Income Tax — "What to deduct"

| Data | Where to collect |
|--------|--------|
| Dependents (personal deduction) | Family relations, income requirement (annual income ≤ 1 million won / earned income ≤ 5 million won) |
| National pension contributions | Insurance corporation/Hometax (fully income-deductible) |
| Noranusan Mutual Aid contributions | Korea Federation of SMEs payment certificate (income deduction) |
| Pension savings/IRP contributions | Financial institution (tax credit) |
| Donations | Donation receipts |
| Other income | Earned/interest/dividend/pension/other income (determination of aggregation vs. separate taxation) |
| Prepaid tax amount | Withheld 3.3%, interim prepayment (refund possible) |

---

## 4. VAT Filing Data

- **Output VAT**: The income data from section 1 above (distinguish taxable/tax-exempt)
- **Input VAT**: Purchase tax invoices + purchases via business card/cash receipts
- Deemed input (tax-exempt agricultural products for restaurants, etc.), credit card sales tax credit, fixed-asset purchases
- When filing VAT on Hometax, **automatic import** of electronic tax invoices, cards, and cash receipts is supported

---

## 5. Collection Priority (Practical Order)

1. **Log in to Hometax → confirm business card/account registration** (register now if you haven't)
2. **Download auto-aggregated sales/purchases from My Hometax** (electronic tax invoices, cards, cash receipts)
3. **Download platform settlement records** (delivery apps, open markets, PG)
4. **Business card/account statements from card companies/banks** (to fill gaps missed by auto-aggregation)
5. **Organize recurring expenses** such as labor costs, rent, and 4 major insurances
6. **Gather deduction documents** (pension, Noranusan, donations, etc.)
7. Organize totals per item in an Excel/worksheet (`templates/worksheet.md`)

> Auto-aggregation ≠ complete. Cash sales, some platforms, and costs without evidence must be filled in by you.
